Welcome to the Solar System

Supposedly, about 4.5 billion years ago, a dense cloud of interstellar gas and dust gave birth to our solar system. A supernova shockwave may have caused the cloud to collapse which then bursted into a spinning disc of material, known as a "solar nebula".

The other planets in our solar system go through a similar procedure. Many spatial rocks began to fuse together, and as they grew in size, they gathered more gravity. They are categorised differently and come in a variety of sizes; like the moons, dwarf planets, and large astral bodies, aka our blue world.

Starting from closest to the Sun, there is Mercury, Venus, Earth and Mars. They are later accompanied by their 4 other friends: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and finally Neptune, in that order.

In their own particular ways, they are all unique. There are a lot of distinctions that we may observe, including the number of moons, their distance from the Sun, their sizes, and their surface climate, etc!

In spite of being confined to our little planet Earth, it is incredibly fascinating and spectacular to be able to hypothesise about space all we want, with only an Internet connection and the ambition to learn.
